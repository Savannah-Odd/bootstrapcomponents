Nintendo Wii

The Nintendo Wii is a video game console that was released in 2006. The primary focus of the release was the wireless controller that had motion sensing ability. The Wii was the first Nintendo console to be able to directly connect to the internet, supporting online games and the “Wii Shop Channel.” The Wii was one of the cheapest games on the market due to its low computational power, making it extremely popular; as of 2022 it was the 5th bestselling home console of all time. The central processing unit is an IBM PowerPC processor, the reduced size of the processor led to a 20% lower power consumption.

The console can read the proprietary discs, 12cm Wii discs, and 8cm GameCube discs but not DVDs or Compact Discs. Games that were available for the Wii were “The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess” and “Super Smash Bros. Brawl,” and because of the motion sensing technology we got games like “Just Dance” and “Wii Sports.”
